How it works

From "When is lunch?" to a calm, predictable day

Set up your first routine in under five minutes — templates included.

1

Build the day in pictures

Add tasks and pick from 130+ icons, your own photos, Pixabay images — or generate icons with Apple Intelligence. Ready-made routine templates get you started fast.

2

Add times & timers

Give each activity a start time and reminder. Launch the full-screen visual timer so your child can literally watch time — with a final countdown and gentle alerts.

3

Let your child take over

Kids see what's now and what's next, and check tasks off themselves. Kid Profiles keep each child's schedules, tasks and progress separate.

4

Celebrate the wins

Every checked box means progress they can feel — and finishing the schedule triggers a confetti celebration that keeps motivation high, even for the boring tasks.

FAQ

Questions parents ask

What is a visual schedule app?
A visual schedule app shows your child's day as a sequence of pictures, icons and timers instead of text. Each activity is a tile the child can see, understand and check off — so kids with autism, ADHD or kids who can't read yet can follow routines independently. Learn more →
Does a visual schedule help children with autism?
Yes — visual supports are one of the most widely recommended strategies for autistic children. Seeing what comes next makes the day predictable, reduces anxiety around transitions and builds independence. Learn more →
How does a routine app help a child with ADHD?
ADHD makes working memory and task initiation hard. Visual Schedule externalizes the routine — icon tiles, a full-screen focus timer, reminders, and a satisfying checkbox with confetti — so the schedule does the remembering, not the child. Learn more →
Can toddlers and non-readers use Visual Schedule?
Yes. Tasks are shown with 130+ icons, your own photos, or Pixabay images, so children who can't read yet can follow their picture schedule on their own. Learn more →
Does Visual Schedule have a visual timer for kids?
Yes. Every task can run a full-screen visual timer with a final countdown plus visual and sound alerts — great for tooth brushing, homework blocks or ending screen time. Learn more →
Can I use it as a first-then board?
Yes. Create a two-task schedule — the "first" activity followed by the preferred "then" activity — so your child sees exactly what good thing follows the hard task. Learn more →
Will it fix our chaotic mornings before school?
Mornings are the #1 use case. Build a picture-based morning routine — wake up, dressed, breakfast, shoes — add timers to each step, and let your child move through the morning without being nagged. Learn more →
Does it help with transitions and meltdowns?
Predictability is the best transition strategy. Kids can always see what's next, and countdown timers warn that an activity is ending — so transitions stop being surprises. Learn more →
Is Visual Schedule free?
Yes — free to download and use on iPhone and iPad, with optional premium features via in-app purchase. Download it free →
Can I set up more than one child?
Yes. Kid Profiles let you create a separate profile for each child, each with their own schedules, tasks and progress — one phone, the whole family organized.
Can teachers use it in the classroom?
Absolutely. Teachers and special educators use it as a digital classroom visual schedule — no more printing and laminating cards. Learn more →
